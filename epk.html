New single "Neighborhood" - Out Now


“Neighborhood renders the angst, the pit in your stomach you feel when you walk around your city during a downturn. I wrote it after reading Mark Fisher’s Capitalist Realism.” Victorino explains.

The song personifies capitalism as a “living organism” that captures and distorts our lives, even our imaginations. It also generally speaks to the advance of gentrification, and a musician’s perspective on the increasing un-livability of Seattle.

The cover art was taken at Ape Caves by Ted Howard and is a visual followup to her first EP Sheer Force of Will.
Biography


Liv Victorino asserts that her quiet, tender songwriting shines brightest against eye-watering distortion and intensity. Following Elliott Smith, The Smashing Pumpkins, and Death Cab for Cutie, Victorino’s band zealously renders her unforgettable melodies while staunchly defending a rock band philosophy. Her set is laden with improvisational forms that deliver a new take on shoegaze, grunge, and baroque-pop.

Once a folk and singer-songwriter project, Victorino reintroduced herself as an alternative rock writer in 2023 with a brand new band. Her packed performance schedule in 2024 has earned an “up next” status in Seattle. After cutting their teeth extensively in the club circuit, the group bubbled up to the top play Idaho’s Treefort Fest, Portland’s Pickathon, and hometown festivals: notably Seattle’s Capitol Hill Block Party, Cloudbreak Music Festival, and Belltown Bloom.

A new EP is set to drop late March. The first single “Neighborhood” debuted the stylistic shift. “Neighborhood” was touted as one of the “best local [releases] of 2024” on NPR’s KUOW, and “best of the PNW 2024” on KEXP’s Audioasis. An upcoming single “Losing” was announced for February.
